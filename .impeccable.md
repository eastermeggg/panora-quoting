## Design Context

### Users
Insurance brokers (courtiers) working at desks during business hours in France. They manage multiple client quotations across several insurers simultaneously. The tool replaces tedious manual work — logging into insurer extranets, filling forms, waiting for quotes, comparing results. Brokers need to feel in command of a complex, high-stakes process where accuracy and completeness matter.

### Brand Personality
**Premium, polished, confident.** Panora should feel like a high-end French professional tool — the kind of interface that signals competence and reliability without flash. Think: a well-made leather portfolio, not a Silicon Valley dashboard. Quiet authority over loud innovation.

### Emotional Goals
- **Confidence** — "I'm in control, nothing is slipping through the cracks"
- **Trust** — "This is accurate and transparent, I can rely on it"
- **Relief** — "This used to take me hours, now it's handled"

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Light — used during business hours in bright office settings
- **Tone**: Refined and restrained. Warm neutrals (off-white #faf8f5, earthy borders #eae7e0) with a deep green accent (#00a272) used sparingly for emphasis. The palette is intentionally muted — confidence comes from precision, not color volume.
- **Typography**: Serif headings (Georgia) for gravitas and editorial warmth; Inter for body text at 13px for data density. The serif/sans pairing signals professionalism without coldness.
- **Anti-references**: Generic SaaS dashboards, neon-accented dark mode tools, overly playful interfaces. This is not a startup toy — it's a professional instrument.
- **Cultural note**: Quality French product — understated sophistication over American tech maximalism. Design should feel considered, not designed.

### Design Principles
1. **Quiet authority** — The interface communicates competence through restraint. Subtle shadows, precise spacing, and muted tones do the work. Nothing shouts.
2. **Information density with clarity** — Brokers need to see a lot at once (multiple insurers, statuses, documents). Pack information tightly but use spacing rhythm and typography hierarchy to keep it scannable.
3. **Trust through transparency** — Every action the AI takes is visible and traceable. Status indicators, timelines, and audit trails are first-class citizens, not afterthoughts.
4. **Precision over decoration** — Every pixel should serve a purpose. No ornamental elements. If an element doesn't help the broker make a decision or track progress, it doesn't belong.
5. **Warmth without softness** — The warm color palette (off-whites, earthy borders) makes the tool approachable without undermining its professional authority. It should feel like a trusted colleague, not a cold machine.
